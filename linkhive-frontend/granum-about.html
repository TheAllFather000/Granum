Our mission

Cut out the
middlemen.

South Africa's food supply chain has too many layers. By the time fresh produce travels from a farm in Limpopo to a spaza shelf in Khayelitsha, it's passed through four or five hands — each one taking a cut. The farmer earns less. The shop owner pays more. The customer gets squeezed.

Granum collapses that chain to one hop. Farmers list directly. Spaza owners order directly. We handle logistics, payments, and trust. Farmers keep 95% of every sale. Spaza owners stock at prices that let them compete.

We're not a charity. We're a platform built on the belief that fair trade, at scale, is the most sustainable form of social impact.

What we stand for

Our values

Five principles that shape every decision we make — from which farmers we onboard to how we handle a late delivery.

🌱

Radical transparency

Every price on Granum shows exactly what the farmer earns and what we take. No hidden markups. No fine print. If we can't defend a number publicly, we don't charge it.

🤝

Community first

We're not building for investors — we're building for the spaza owner who wakes up at 5am, and the farmer who hasn't had a fair buyer in years. Their wins are our wins.

⚡

Speed as respect

When someone's livelihood depends on stock arriving on time, speed is not a feature — it's a form of respect. We obsess over 24-hour delivery because it matters.

🔒

Trust by default

Verified badges aren't gatekeeping — they're earned. We verify every farmer and manufacturer so spaza owners can order with confidence, not anxiety.

♻️

Local, always

We prioritise suppliers within 200km of delivery areas. Shorter supply chains mean fresher food, lower emissions, and money that stays in the community.

Where we come from

Born in a township,
built for scale.

Granum started as a conversation between a spaza owner in Khayelitsha and a computer science student from UCT. The spaza owner was paying R28 per 5kg bag of maize meal from a wholesaler in town. The same bag cost R22 at Pick n Pay two streets over — a store she couldn't stock from.

"Why is it cheaper to buy retail than wholesale? Something is broken."

— The conversation that started Granum

We built a prototype in three weeks. Within a month, five spaza shops were ordering. Within six months, we had 87 suppliers on the platform. The problem was real. The demand was there. We just needed to build the bridge.
